    Tumbleweed Appetite Settings
    About these settings
    ? The percent drop in market price for Tumbleweed to stretch funds over so it can continue to trade. This protection recalculates whenever a sell order executes.
    ? The difference in price between subsequent buys. For example, if a buy executes at $185/LTC, and the BDI is $0.20, the next buy order will be placed at $184.80.
    ? The difference in price between a buy order and its associated sell. For example, if a buy executes at $185/LTC, and the PI is $0.20, The sell order will be placed at $185.20.
    ? The percent of per sell profit to realize in the base currency. For example, if this was set at 20% and quote profit was $0.05, then $0.01 worth of the base currency will not be included in the sell order quantity, and quote profit would be $0.04.
    ? The amount of funds in your exchange's quote currency account to keep separate from Tumbleweed's trading activity, e.g., USD to hold back from trading in you're trading LTC-USD.
